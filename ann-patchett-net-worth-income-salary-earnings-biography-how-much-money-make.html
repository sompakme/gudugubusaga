Ann Patchett (born December 2, 1963) is a writer from the United States. Her book Bel Canto won the Orange Prize for Fiction as well as the PEN/Faulkner Award in 2002, and she was named a finalist for both awards. In addition to Run, The Patron Saint of Liars, Taft, State of Wonder, and The Magician’s Assistant, which was nominated for the Orange Prize, Patchett has written many additional works. The Nashville Banner Tennessee Writer of the Year Award was given to her in 1994, and she has won a Guggenheim Fellowship.
