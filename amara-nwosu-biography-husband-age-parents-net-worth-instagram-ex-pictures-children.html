Career

Amarachi Nwosu‘s artistic journey started in Washington, D.C., tracing her heritage to Nigeria and Ghana. However, she had received formal training in International Communications, and her talent for photography developed naturally. While studying at Temple University, she spent a year in Tokyo, which exposed her to various viewpoints and ignited her love for storytelling.

Through Budweiser’s Budx platform, she successfully arranged a two-day exhibition, workshop, panel, concert, and party in Nigeria. This event sought to bridge the gap between America’s golden hip-hop period and Nigeria’s present hip-hop culture. She coordinated the forthcoming show for Nigerian-American hip-hop documentarian Chi Modu as part of her Melanin Unscripted platform.

In addition to her talent in photography, Nwosu has also demonstrated her artistic abilities in directing short films for Nike. These films highlight the strength and influence of women in sports within Nigeria.

Furthermore, Amarachi Nwosu‘s creative direction and brand management skills have significantly promoted the Afrobeats and Afro-fusion sound. Through collaborations with artists such as Mr Eazi, Yxng Bane, and Nonso Amadi, she has helped elevate these genres to new heights. Additionally, Nwosu was honored to be the tour photographer for Childish Gambino‘s highly acclaimed “This is America” tour.
