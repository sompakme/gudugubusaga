Furthermore, are stairwells included in the square footage calculation twice?

Stairs: Both the runs/treads and the landings are included in the overall square footage calculations. They are measured as a portion of the floor “from whence they descend,” and as a result, in a normal two-story house with a basement, they are typically tallied twice.

In the same way, does the square footage encompass all of the floors?

The square footage of a house only covers the first and second floors on the ground level. It does not include the basement, garage, attic, or other similar areas. The open sections on the second level that have a view of the first floor are also excluded from this rule.

Are finished basements included in the square footage calculation?

Basements, dens, and other below-grade rooms are not often included in a home’s total square footage calculation. Finished basements, for example, cannot be included in the calculation of a home’s Gross Living Area (GLA), although they may be included in the listing’s total area.
