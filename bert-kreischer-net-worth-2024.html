Early Life and Education

Bert Kreischer was born in Tampa, Florida, and attended a private Jesuit high school before enrolling at Florida State University (FSU). At FSU, he was recognized by Rolling Stone as the top partyer at the Number One Party School in the country.

Comedy Career

Kreischer moved to New York City after college to pursue a career in comedy. He quickly gained recognition for his wild storytelling and unique comedic style. His stand-up specials, including “The Machine,” “Secret Time,” and “Hey Big Boy,” have been well-received by audiences and critics alike.

Television Career

In addition to his stand-up career, Kreischer has also made a name for himself in television. He hosted the reality show “Hurt Bert” on FX, where he took on various daring challenges. He also hosted “Bert the Conqueror” on the Travel Channel and “Trip Flip” on the same network.

Acting Career

Kreischer has also dabbled in acting, appearing in episodes of popular television shows like “Workaholics” and “The Shield.” He also lent his voice to the animated series “The Life & Times of Tim.”

Podcasting

Another source of Kreischer’s income is his successful podcast, “Bertcast.” The podcast features interviews with fellow comedians and entertainers and has a large following.

Book Sales

Merchandise Sales

Real Estate Investments

Endorsements and Sponsorships
