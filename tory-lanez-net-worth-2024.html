Early Life and Career Beginnings

Tory Lanez was born in Brampton, Ontario, Canada. He developed an interest in music at a young age and began rapping at the age of nine. After a series of personal challenges, Lanez moved to the United States where he pursued his music career more seriously. His first mixtape, ‘T.L 2 T.O’, was released in 2009 and marked the beginning of his professional career.

Rise to Fame

Lanez’s breakthrough came in 2014 with the release of his mixtape ‘Lost Cause’, which garnered significant attention. This led to a record deal with Mad Love Records and Interscope Records. His debut studio album, ‘I Told You’, was released in 2016 and peaked at number four on the Billboard 200 chart.

Continued Success

Following the success of his debut album, Lanez released several other successful albums including ‘Memories Don’t Die’, ‘Love Me Now?’, and ‘Chixtape 5’. His music spans several genres including hip hop, R&B, and dancehall, showcasing his versatility as an artist.
